# What Goes Where?

!!! info "Important note and disclaimer"
    This document is intended as a courtesy to provide an overview of platforms administered by Digital Scholarship Infrastructure (DSI), their features, and relevant policies. It is derived from departmental and platform policies; in case of disagreement, platform and departmental policies take precedent. This document does not guarantee that the eligibility of any documents or publications for inclusion in DSI platforms. Please contact DSI at diginit@yorku.ca to discuss the suitability of any material for inclusion on these platforms.

## About

### Purpose

The What Goes Where (WGW) project aims to harmonize and resolve ambiguities in DSI practice around which types of materials would be published and/or preserved on which platforms. It is not intended to supersede platform-level policy or the work of the Digital Scholarship Working Group (DSWG) and the RASCI matrices (Responsible, Accountable, Support, Consulted, Informed) it developed; rather, it provides practice guidelines.

The goal of the project is two-fold:

1. To provide decision-making support for DSI members, YUL colleagues, and external users to understand which type of material belongs in which DSI platform;

2. To help these same groups understand and compare the affordances of each platform.

This document is the project’s first output; we expect to derive more specific outputs tailored to user groups in the future.

### History

The What Goes Where Working Group (WGWWG) formed in Fall 2023. Initial membership consisted of chair Tomasz Mrozewski, Kris Joseph, Harriet South, Anna St. Onge, and Priscilla Carmini. After the departure of Anna St. Onge and Priscilla Carmini, it became a department-level DSI project in summer 2024 featuring input from all members.

The group started by reviewing policy documents for each DSI platform and doing a partial content audit of each platform. Then, we reviewed our findings for overlaps and ambiguity, then arranged our findings into a matrix of affordances. We then drafted and refined the matrices into their present form over summer and fall 2024.

### Past practice

This document is meant to be forward-looking and does not describe past practice. Legacy practices should not necessarily be taken as a guide for future decisions. Prior decisions may have been made on the availability of platforms, on the capacity of those platforms at the time, and in the absence of harmonized departmental policy. Likewise, this document and the guidelines therein are likely to change in the future.

### Discretionary decision-making

This document should be taken as a general practice guideline. We expect edge cases and unanticipated situations to arise in which the department members can and should exercise discretion about where to publish materials. Discretionary decision making should not be arbitrary and should consider the mission of the department, the stated purpose and policies of the platforms, and the RASCI matrices developed by the DPWG. Ideally, discretionary decisions will be discussed by the department before being finalized.

## Platform summaries

### YorkSpace

Contents will be preserved in perpetuity beyond the life of the platform. In exceptional circumstances, content may be removed. Stable versions only. Versions are visible to admin and only the most recent is available to public. Primarily used for author deposit of scholarship and for electronic theses and dissertations (ETD) ETD / Masters Research Papers (MRP) repository. May also be used for grey literature, some open educational resources (OERs), and other materials per DSI discretion. Primarily used for self-deposit by faculty authors and graduate students, as well as for ETD and MRPs. Authors control the content. DSI may make changes to metadata or content in exceptional circumstances. Mediated deposit by DSI upon author request. Some authorized external users can deposit on behalf of departments. Material can only be publicly available. YorkSpace is discovery-oriented and is indexed and harvested by various discovery tools. It allows for DOIs, short URLs, basic metrics, multimedia upload.

### YUDL

Contents will be preserved in perpetuity beyond the life of the platform. In exceptional circumstances, content may be removed. Stable versions only. Versions are visible to admin and only the most recent is available to public. Primarily used for digitized or born digital cultural heritage objects rather than scholarly works. Primarily used for digitized or born digital cultural heritage objects regardless of author’s affiliation. Content is subject to deposit agreements with DSI or ASC. Content is not expected to change once deposited, although metadata may be added or corrected. Mediated deposit by DSI upon owner request. Material is either open to the public or restricted to authorized account holders. YUDL is preservation-oriented and designed for multimedia works. 

### YDJ

Contents will be preserved for the life of the platform. All journals are encouraged to register with Scholars Portal Journals for perpetual preservation. Stable versions only. Versions are numbered and are accessible publicly. Primarily used for the original publication of scholarly, creative, and academic business under the guidance of external editorial teams. All works are subject to the individual journals’ submission and editorial policies. Journal editors control the content. DSI may make changes to metadata or content in exceptional circumstances. Content is submitted by authors to the editors for approval and publication. Depending on editorial policy, authors submit directly to OJS or to editors for ingest. Material is mostly open; some legacy journals are toll-access. YDJ is discovery-oriented and journals may be indexed or harvested by indexers. It allows for DOIs, ORCID iDs, bibliometrics, and the integration of content and multimedia hosted elsewhere.

### Scalar

Contents will be preserved for the life of the platform. Users may also remove their content. Works in progress supported. Content authoring possible on-platform. Versions are visible to admin and only the most recent is available to public. Primarily used as an authoring platform for generating outputs to be hosted elsewhere, such as YorkSpace or eCampus Ontario. Primarily used by faculty to author works for hosting elsewhere. Authors control the content. DSI may make changes to metadata or content in exceptional circumstances. Authors create and ingest content as required. Material may be open or restricted to specific user groups with accounts. Pressbooks is an authoring tool that permits the integration of locally hosted and outside content and multimedia.  

### Pressbooks

Contents will be preserved for the life of the platform. Users may also remove their content. Works in progress supported. Content authoring possible on-platform. Versions are visible to admin and only the most recent is available to public. Primarily used as an authoring platform for generating outputs to be hosted elsewhere, such as YorkSpace or eCampus Ontario. Primarily used by faculty to author works for hosting elsewhere. Authors control the content. DSI may make changes to metadata or content in exceptional circumstances. Authors create and ingest content as required. Material may be open or restricted to specific user groups with accounts. Pressbooks is an authoring tool that permits the integration of locally hosted and outside content and multimedia.

### Archivematica

Contents will be preserved in perpetuity beyond the life of the platform. In exceptional circumstances, content may be removed. Stable versions only. Versions are visible to only admin. Primarily used for digitized or born digital cultural heritage objects under the custody and control of ASC. Content is subject to deposit agreements with ASC. Content is not expected to change once deposited, although metadata may be added or corrected. Mediated deposit by DSI or ASC. Material is restricted to authorized account holders (Nick Ruest in DSI and ASC archivists).

### Web archives

Contents will be preserved in perpetuity beyond the life of the platform. In exceptional circumstances, content may be removed. Stable versions only. All versions are visible to the public. Primarily used to capture and preserve web content that falls under York University's Common Records Schedule, as well as occasional crawls of York University web properties where resources can permit so. Content is not expected to change once deposited, although metadata may be added or corrected. Web crawls and deposit by DSI upon request. Material is open to the public.

## Indexing – “Is deposited material indexed and discoverable elsewhere?”

### Summary

* **YorkSpace**: Materials are indexed by web crawlers. Materials are usually automatically indexed in Google and Google Scholar. The Electronic Thesis and Dissertation Collection in YorkSpace is indexed by Library and Archive of Canada. Scholarly works discoverable/indexed by Discover York Academics. 
* **YUDL**: Materials are indexed by web crawlers, OAI-PHM, and RSS/ATOM feeds. 
* **YDJ**: Materials are indexed by web crawlers. Materials are usually automatically indexed in Google Scholar. Indexing in academic databases is subject to application or agreement between editors and vendors. 
* **Scalar**: Materials are indexed by web crawlers, but the platform does not present enriched metadata in the same fashion as other repositories, so the discoverability is not as good as it could be. 
* **Pressbooks**: Materials are indexed by web crawlers. A few items in Pressbooks have been indexed in Google Scholar but this does not seem to be the rule, even for scholarly material
* **Archivematica**: Materials are not indexed
* **Web archiving**: The web archive homepage is indexed by web crawlers

## Copyright and licensing – “Who own the copyright for materials on this platform?”

### Summary

* **YorkSpace**: Authors need to sign a non-exclusive distribution licence; copyright may belong to authors or to publishers with provision for author self-deposit
* **YUDL**: Copyright and licensing vary according to each object
* **YDJ**: Copyright and licensing policies vary according to each journal’s editorial policy
* **Scalar**: Copyright and licensing vary according to each project
* **Pressbooks**: Copyright and licensing vary according to each project
* **Archivematica**: Copyright and licensing vary according to each object
* **Web archiving**: Copyright belongs to York University, unless specified for specific web pages; no license applies unless stipulated on individual pages

* Zotero libraries (including exports) 
* Faculty-authored reports for outside organizations 
* Individual poster or conference papers that are deposited without being part of a collection or proceedings 
* Pre-prints that have not been accepted for publication elsewhere 
* 3D models related to scholarly work
